The German Economy is on a Major Downslide

Updated October 10, 2014

The Bundestag will cut its economic growth forecasts next week, according to Reuters, citing German government sources. Berlin will sharply trim its expectations for economic growth to about 1.25pc for both 2014 and 2015, as compared to earlier predictions of a 1.8pc and 2pc growth. In recent weeks of reports, it has been concluded on Thursday that exports had collapsed to their lowest levels since the depths of the financial crisis. This week the International Monetary Fund slashed its German growth forecasts to 1.4pc for 2014 and 1.5pc for 2015 and leading research institutes- known as Germany's "wise men" - lowered their forecasts to 1.3pc and 1.2pc. The "wise men" added that the German economy is now in “stagnation” and unemployment will likely rise next year. “There are no signs of the long-awaited recovery yet. Corporate investment fell in the second quarter and there is hardly any evidence to suggest that this cautious approach to investment will change in the near future,” the "wise men" said. Senior government officials will meet on Monday to refine the forecasts to be announced by Economy Minister Sigmar Gabriel on Tuesday. This will serve as the basis for new tax estimates.
